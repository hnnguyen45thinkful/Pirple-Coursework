Vegetarian (Vegan) West African Peanut Soup

This recipe was unique because a peanut soup recipe from West African sounds digusting and weird. But after eating this in a restaurant this was so creamy and comforting, and LASTY, this is a spicy vegan soup. With all vegetables and proteins your body needs made with a simple combination of peanut butter, tomato paste and collard greens, this soup comes together quickly and would be a great weekday meal in the evening or on a Sunday night. The reason I like this complex and confusing recipe is because I love bold and SPICY flavors TONS of amounts of ginger and garlic which has a savory smell.

List of Ingredients (Try to Get Organic):

I copied and pasted the images/icons on the website https://www.emojicopy.com/ and
  • 6 cups Low Sodium Vegetable Broth
  • 1 whole round Red Onion
  • 1 or 2 LARGE Carrot(s) 🥕
  • 1 or 2 LARGE Sweet Potatoe(s) or Regular White Potatoe(s) 🍠 🥔
  • Any other vegetable(s) that is TASTY to you 🍆 🥦 🍄 🌽 🥒
  • 2 tablespoons peeled and minced fresh Ginger
  • 4 or more cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt (sea salt or himalayan salt)
  • 1 bunch collard greens (or kale), ribs removed and leaves chopped into 1-inch strips
  • 3/4 cup unsalted peanut butter (chunky or smooth) 🥜
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste (organic or regular) 🍅
  • Hot sauce, like sriracha (AKA rooster sauce) OR A bunch of Hot Peppers (Jalapeno, Sereno or my favorite HABANERO peppers) 🌶️

Directions How to Cook:

  1. In a medium Dutch oven, chinese wok or stock pot or any LARGE size pot, bring the broth to a boil. Then, add the onion, ginger, garlic and salt. Cook on medium-low heat for 15-20 minutes.
  2. In a medium-sized, heat-safe mixing bowl, combine the peanut butter and tomato paste, then transfer 1 to 2 cups of the hot stock to the bowl. Whisk the mixture together until smooth (NO CHUNKS) 🥣 , then pour the peanut mixture back into the soup and mix well. Stir in the collard greens and season the soup with hot sauce and/or peppers to taste 🥘.
  3. Simmer for about 15 to 20 or more minutes on medium-low heat, stirring often UNTIL the vegetables are fully cooked and soft. Serve over cooked white or brown rice 🍚🍚🍚🍚🍚 if you’d like, and top with a sprinkle of chopped peanuts 🍲.
